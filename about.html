Built by someone who works in logistics every day

Why FreightGauge exists

Most small ecommerce businesses send thousands of parcels a year and have no real idea where the money goes. They get an invoice, pay it, and move on — because pulling it apart feels hard, and there's no obvious person to ask.

Freight cost analysis isn't complicated. But it does require knowing what to look for — how carriers structure their charges, how dimensional weight billing works, what a reasonable fuel levy looks like, and where typical SMB businesses leak margin without realising it.

FreightGauge exists to make that analysis accessible. A fixed-price, no-fluff health check — with clear findings and a practical action plan — delivered to business owners who have better things to do than spend a week building pivot tables.

"I wanted to build something that felt like getting advice from someone who actually works in freight — not a consultant quoting hourly rates or a carrier trying to upsell you."

What FreightGauge is — and isn't

FreightGauge is a productised service. It has a fixed scope, a fixed price, and a fixed turnaround. It's designed to be fast, independent, and useful — not comprehensive consulting.

It won't negotiate your carrier contract for you, implement a new shipping strategy, or replace your 3PL. What it will do is give you the data and direction you need to have those conversations from a position of knowledge rather than guesswork.
