The problem

Shipping costs are the last thing most businesses actually look at

Not a consultant quoting hourly rates. Not a carrier trying to upsell you. Independent freight analysis, from someone who works inside the industry every day.

01

Carrier invoices are opaque

Fuel levies, remote area surcharges, dimensional weight billing. Most businesses accept the invoice total without questioning what's inside it.

02

Free shipping isn't free

Every order where you absorb the freight cost is margin leaving the business. Most owners don't know the actual subsidy amount until they see it laid out in a report.

03

Packaging drives more cost than you think

Carriers bill on volumetric weight, not just dead weight. An oversized box can cost you significantly more than the product inside warrants.

04

No benchmark, no visibility

Without data, there's nothing to negotiate with. FreightGauge turns your invoice data into a clear picture you can act on and take to your carrier.

A typical ecommerce business shipping 300 orders/month across AusPost and Couriers Please is often paying 8–15% more than their rate card suggests, once you account for volumetric weight upgrades, fuel levy compounding, and remote area charges applied to non-remote postcodes. On a $10,000/month freight bill, that is $800–$1,500 a month you did not need to spend.
