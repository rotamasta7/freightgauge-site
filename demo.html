Action plan
10 ranked recommendations

Each card maps directly to a section of the full report. Click to expand the finding and specific next steps.

1
Right-size packaging on top 12 SKUsReduce volumetric billing exposure
$370/mo
Medium effort
⌄
71% of consignments are charged on volumetric weight — 23 pts above industry average. The top 12 SKUs are all over-packaged: avg dead weight 0.68 kg, avg chargeable 1.24 kg. Right-sizing these alone covers ~64% of total DIM billing exposure.
Audit carton dimensions for the top 12 SKUs against AusPost’s cubic weight calculator
Target chargeable weight within 15% of dead weight — eliminates most of the surcharge
Request cubic weight report from AusPost or CP to validate before ordering new cartons
2
Reduce Express service usageMove standard domestic to Economy
$340/mo
Low effort
⌄
42% of AusPost consignments are shipped Express. 61% of those were placed before 2pm on weekdays — sufficient lead time for Economy to meet a 2–3 business day expectation. There is no evidence customers are selecting Express at checkout.
Default all non-metro domestic orders to AusPost Economy where no express preference is indicated
Add Express as a paid upgrade at checkout (~$4.50 surcharge covers the cost)
Review pick/pack cutoff — if orders go out by 3pm, Economy is almost always sufficient
3
Raise free shipping threshold to $90Reduce subsidy from 68% to ~52% of orders
$290/mo
Low effort
⌄
At $75, 68% of orders qualify for free shipping — a $3,510/month net subsidy. Modelling shows raising to $90 reduces qualifying orders to ~52%, saving $290/month while affecting only the tail of low-value orders.
Update the free shipping threshold in your ecommerce platform from $75 to $90
Monitor conversion rate for 30 days — rollback trigger if conversion drops more than 2%
Add a progress bar at checkout (“$X more for free shipping”) to offset any cart abandonment
4
Recover remote area surchargesPass-through or pre-screen flagged postcodes
$580 one-off
Low effort
⌄
$1,540 in remote area surcharges across 47 unique postcodes over 3 months. 31 of those postcodes are on the carriers’ published remote lists — meaning they were predictable at checkout. A postcode validator enables the surcharge to be shown before purchase.
Cross-reference destination postcodes against AusPost and CP remote area lists (updated quarterly)
Implement a remote area surcharge line item at checkout using a postcode lookup
For B2B orders, add remote area recovery to your outbound freight terms
5
Negotiate CP ratesVolume now warrants a formal rate review
$180/mo
Medium effort
⌄
At 440 consignments/month, CP volume is above the informal threshold for rate negotiation (typically 300+). Current rates appear to be standard retail tier. A formal review with a 12-month commitment could yield 4–7% off base freight — conservatively $180/month at current volume.
Contact your CP account manager and request a formal rate review — reference the 440 con/month volume
Bring 3 months of zone and weight mix data to support the negotiation
Counter any volume commitment with a 6-month trial clause to retain flexibility
